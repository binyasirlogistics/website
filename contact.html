Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our dispatching services

What is the difference between a dispatcher and a broker?

A dispatcher works directly for you, the carrier, to find loads and negotiate with brokers on your behalf. A broker, on the other hand, works with shippers to find carriers to move their freight. As your dispatcher, we represent your interests exclusively and work to maximize your revenue.

How do you find loads for your carriers?

We use multiple load boards, including DAT, Truckstop.com, and 123Loadboard, as well as our established network of broker relationships. We also maintain direct relationships with some shippers for access to exclusive freight opportunities.

Do I need to sign a long-term contract?

No, our service agreements are month-to-month. We believe in earning your business through performance rather than locking you into a long-term contract.

How are your fees calculated?

Our fees are calculated as a percentage of the gross revenue for each load we dispatch for you. The percentage varies based on the service package you choose, ranging from 7% to 10% for most carriers.

Can I choose which loads to accept?

Absolutely. We present load options to you based on your preferences, but you always have the final say on which loads you accept. We believe in giving you control over your business while providing expert guidance.

What documentation do I need to get started?

To get started, we'll need copies of your MC Authority, insurance certificates (liability and cargo), W-9 form, and a completed carrier profile form. These documents help us set you up with brokers and begin finding loads that match your capabilities.
